Microsoft Academic Alliance

Are you a computer science or computer engineering student in need of tools such as Visual Studio, SQL Server, Windows Server, Visio, Windows 10/8/7 license, or other microsoft applications? If you are enrolled in a computer science course, you are eligible to obtain many Microsoft development tools free of charge for academic use. You can get these for free through the CSE deparment by filling out This Form and turning it in to room JB 351.

Upcoming Events:


Study Marathon

Saturday, March 17, 2018 @ 12:00 - 4:30 pm

Jack Brown Hall, Third Floor, Room JB 357-360

Price: $10 general admission, $5 for members

Ready for Finals Week? The CSE Club is opening the computer labs so that you can continue studying with your peers during the last Saturday of Winter quarter. Bring your books and your friends and we’ll have a fun time prepping for the most stressful week of the quarter. Also, the club library will be open for anyone to use while studying for their CSE class.



Computer History Museum Trip

Wednesday, March 28, 2018 @ 6:00 am - 1:00 am (the next day)

CSUSB to Mountain View, CA and back again

Hold on to your backpacks! Our most ambitious trip of the year is coming around the corner. The CSE Cub has pulled together a one day trip to the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, CA. We will spend a few hours looking through amazing displays of the history of computing. The museum has everything from the IBM 1041 to groundbreaking software to autonomous vehicles. After the museum tour, we will head on over to the Google campus for some Ultimate Frisbee on the grass. Note that the drive to Mountain View is at least seven hours, so it will be a long day. Please plan to bring snacks with you and $20 for dinner. The club will also be bringing some snacks for the trip. There are only 15 spots available, so RSVP on our Google Form ASAP.
