Anouncements:

2018-2019 Officer Application

Interested in becoming a club officer for the next school year? This is one of the greatest extracurricular activities you can participate in on a school campus. If you have a vision of what you could see the club doing next year, this is the best way to get your ideas out and start acting on them. These positions teach you skills that employers want, but nobody really talks about or practices. These skills include creating and sending emails, working with a close team of peers, managing projects and members, and learning how to have fun with all that going on. Take a moment and seriously consider this resume-boosting door of opportunity. Fill out this Form to apply.

Microsoft Academic Alliance

Are you a computer science or computer engineering student in need of tools such as Visual Studio, SQL Server, Windows Server, Visio, Windows 10/8/7 license, or other microsoft applications? If you are enrolled in a computer science course, you are eligible to obtain many Microsoft development tools free of charge for academic use. You can get these for free through the CSE deparment by filling out This Form and turning it in to room JB 351.

Upcoming Events:


Vivado: Full Adder Workshop

Thursday, April 19th, 2018 @11:00 am

Jack Brown Hall, Third floor, Room 359

Learn how to use Vivado. Vivado is a software suite produced by Xilinx for synthesis and analysis of HDL designs, superseding Xilinx ISE with additional features for system on a chip development and high-level synthesis. Join us as we learn and explore Vivado.



CSUSB Tech Fair

Friday, April 20, 2018 @ 11:00 am - 4:00 pm

Jack Brown Hall, First Floor, Room JB 122

Register on EventBrite

Come join SWiCS, InfoSec, WiE, and the CSE club in our first CSUSB Tech Fair! We will have workshops on different programming languages, circuit building/soldering, and talks from guest speakers. This event is aimed at people of all skill levels, beginner to advanced, and there will be something to learn for everyone. The speakers at the event will be from a variety of companies within the tech industry. Make Them Mainstream will be at the event to talk about “How to be a better tutor,” a great skill for anyone interested! We will also be doing a live build of a BOSEbuild bluetooth speaker as well as giving one away, a $150 dollar value!! We will also be giving away two soldering stations!

The event is on April 20th, 11am-4pm at CSU San Bernardino. Be sure to RSVP here, on Eventbrite. Once you RSVP keep your receipt! You will be asked for this (on your cell phone) at the door on the day of the event, this is when you will pay your $20 entry fee. Included in your ticket price is a FREE giveaway ticket to win a $150 BOSEbuild speaker or a soldering station! If you choose to purchase more giveaway tickets to increase your chances of winning they will be sold for $3 cash per extra ticket.

The event will also have a make-and-take element, meaning participants get to make a piece of tech themselves and take it home with them! Come check it out! It's a great way to meet other students in the technology field and to improve your skills or learn a new one! Partially funded by the CSUSB Cyber Security Center.
