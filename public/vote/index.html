President
Dylan Allbee

Friends of the Computer Science & Engineering Club,

In the last year we have seen overwhelming growth within the club. What was once a handful of individuals is now a community of more than seventy members joined together by our common interests. Our library has grown to include scores of books, we've funded an exciting and ongoing quadrotor project, and just this quarter we have tutored dozens of students. We've made real progress, but we've got more work to do.

As many of you may have noticed, our meetings and events have seen declining attendence. We've struggled to handle changes within officership throughout the year. Events have been canceled and promises have been left unfulfilled. Finally, we are not currently fully ACM compliant. This is unacceptable, and we must strive to do better to create the club that the students of CSUSB deserve.

Over the past year, I have held the positions of Webmaster and Treasurer. In these positions, I have done my best to act as an objective member of the board who seeks to make sure nothing goes awry. While this attitude has been instrumental in assuring that the club stay within certain bounds, it's time for me to step up.

I intend to fix these problems that I've outlined. I have a detailed plan for achieving this, and I will work to the best of my ability to ensure that the club reaches it's full potential. Please contact me if you have any questions.


Vice President
Mark Swoope

If you elect me as one of these officers, I will strive to ensure that we embrace more knowledge in the field of computer science and engineering. This club should become stronger academically and serve as a beacon to those who wish to succeed. Our members should be highly involved and motivated more about computer science and engineering than any other students in the department. The problem I see with our department is that a lot of students are not motivated by the subject. People come to class, do their work, and then go home. I rarely see students stick around after class and talk about computer science or computer engineering. I rarely see students engage in feedback during the lectures and a lot of them struggle during the labs. As a club we need to boost the spirit of the department and get people interested in pursuing and sharing knowledge in this field. The CSE club needs to be more than a social club, it needs to be a source of academic inspiration and prosperity.

By running for these roles, I also strive to ensure fairness and non-partisan policies made for the club by me and the other officers. I will make sure to scrutinize every decision brought up amongst the officers and make sure that it is constitutional and sound. I do not want to see a mindless agreement between the officers just because they are friends. I want to see progress through everyone's participation and diversity of ideas.

Andrew Brinker

CSE Club is doing well. In the last year we've watched our membership, our finances, and our offerings increase greatly, and we are now presented with an opportunity to seriously improve the lives of our members.

Several quarters ago I started a peer-to-peer volunteer tutoring program, first just me, and then with only two tutors. We know have six tutors, covering a great majority of the classes offered by this department, and students are noticing. Attendance at tutoring hours and office hours has exploded. My first goal for the next year is to expand and improve tutoring, including getting more tutors, providing better advertising, and making resources available online for a number of department courses.

I also want to start a club workshop program, where students can come at regular times to learn important and useful skills they may not get in class.

I'll also work to expand and improve the projects program, making projects more visible, and more consistent.

Finally, I'll work with the Webmaster to make more resources available online, including club forms, lightning talk videos, and detailed project information.

My goals for next year are to build on the good things we're already doing. To make the club more visible, and to provide more resources to students in the School of Computer Science and Engineering. If elected for next year, I will work as hard as I can to continue the club's success, and to make life better for you.

 Anthony Sterrett

'Twas brillig, and the slithy toves
Did gyre and gimble in the wabe;
All mimsy were the borogoves,
And the mome raths outgrabe.

"Beware the Jabberwock, my son!
The jaws that bite, the claws that catch!
Beware the Jubjub bird, and shun
The frumious Bandersnatch!"

He took his vorpal sword in hand:
Long time the manxome foe he sought—
So rested he by the Tumtum tree,
And stood awhile in thought.

And as in uffish thought he stood,
The Jabberwock, with eyes of flame,
Came whiffling through the tulgey wood,
And burbled as it came!

One, two! One, two! and through and through
The vorpal blade went snicker-snack!
He left it dead, and with its head
He went galumphing back.

"And hast thou slain the Jabberwock?
Come to my arms, my beamish boy!
O frabjous day! Callooh! Callay!"
He chortled in his joy.
