Computer Science & Engineering Club is a club at CSU, San Bernardino devoted to computer science, computer engineering, and computer systems. Interested in us? Feel free to checkout our Facebook Group, Google Group, or GitHub account. We also have a page on the CSE Department website. The club office is in Jack Brown room 540.

Membership Application

Our club is open to everyone, and we encourage you to join! You can do so by filling out the membership application and either turning it into an officer, or dropping it off in our office. We do have a membership fee of $10 per quarter. At this time we can only accept either cash or check.

Projects

As a member of the CSE Club, you are able to participate in any of the four club projects we have. Participating in these projects is a great way to broaden and improve your abilities in our field. One of the most important things that you can have on your resume is a portfolio of work that you have done outside of university classes.

As part of a project team, you'll be tackling challenging engineering problems under the guidance of a knowledgeable team leader. You'll learn how to apply the knowledge you've learned in the classroom in real world situations, all while you're making new friends and building connections.

We do ask a bit of you if you choose to join a project:

  • Bring an enthusiastic and open mindset.
  • Be willing to commit 3-5 hours per week to the project.
  • Let your team leader know if your situation changes and time can't be committed.
  • Assertively ask questions and provide feedback.

Each team will be capped at 8-12 members, depending on the project. There will be a two-week trial period where anyone can participate, while the team leader will select the team over those two weeks. The criteria for selection is participation, willingness to learn, and basic knowledge of the relevant skills. To be clear, your current capabilities are only a very small part of the criteria, so don't be discouraged if you don't have adequate knowledge of the subject.

To join a project, email the team leader with "<project name> - Join Request" in the subject line. In the body, be sure to include your name, student ID, why you are interested in the project, and any relevant skills or experience you may have.

If you'd like to see more projects, feel free to contact us with suggestions. We're always looking for new and exciting projects to bring to the club!
